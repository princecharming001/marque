# Transitions — taxonomy AND restraint (rules with numbers)

## The default is a clean cut
- A hard cut is the right transition ~90% of the time in short-form; effects must EARN
  their place by adding meaning, not energy for its own sake.
- Never two styled transitions back-to-back; minimum ~3s (90 frames) between styled
  transitions, and at most 2–3 styled transitions in a 30s piece.

## Cut grammar (invisible edits first)
- **J-cut** (audio of the NEXT shot leads ~8–14 frames): momentum — use INTO b-roll and
  new beats; the ear arrives before the eye.
- **L-cut** (audio of the PREVIOUS shot lingers ~8–14 frames): weight — use out of an
  emotional or emphatic line so it lands over the next visual.
- **Cut on action**: cut mid-movement (gesture, turn, reach) — motion masks the edit.
  Prefer a cut 2–4 frames AFTER the action starts.
- Match the split-edit frequency to tone: fast/energetic content → shorter overlaps and
  more frequent cuts; story/education → longer L-cuts, fewer seams.

## Styled transition taxonomy (when one IS earned)
- **fade_black** (12f): topic close / act break. Never inside a continuous thought.
- **fade_white** (12f): reveal / flashback flavor; bright content only.
- **flash** (3–6f, ≤0.10 peak): beat accent on a hard cut — entertainment/fast_cuts only,
  rate-limited ≥45 output frames apart (mirrors the render's FastCuts rule).
- Whip/zoom/spin effects read amateurish in talking-head content — do not request them.

## Section boundaries
- Pattern interrupts (styled transition, punch-in, b-roll swap) belong at the HOOK and at
  SECTION boundaries only — not on every sentence.

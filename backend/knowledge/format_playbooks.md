# Format playbooks — one operational section per EDIT_FORMATS key

## talking_head
- The take is the spine: cut ONLY filler, dead-air >350ms, flubs. Never chop mid-sentence.
- Exactly ONE punch-in (1.05–1.10) on the single most load-bearing line — not the hook,
  not the CTA. Entry AND exit ease ~8 frames.
- Captions 3-word phrases, lower-middle third; keyword highlight on emphasis words.
- 0 styled transitions by default; J-cut the cold open if the first word starts late.
- SFX budget 3–5 per 30s; no music unless asked (voice clarity wins).

## talking_head_broll
- Spine = talking head; cutaways on CONCRETE nouns/actions only, J-cut lead ~12 frames,
  hold 2–3s (60–90f), spacing ≥3s (90f), NEVER over the hook (first 90f) or CTA (last 60f).
- Match b-roll palette/energy to the a-roll; prefer own-media over stock.
- One whoosh per cutaway maximum; cut back to face ON the next spoken emphasis.

## recap_music
- Music IS the spine: every cut beat-timed; cadence 1–2s (30–60f), no shot >3s.
- Hook visual + bass hit at f0–15; one riser into the strongest moment; flash accents
  (≤0.10 peak) allowed on downbeat cuts, ≥45f apart.
- Speech minimal — captions carry any words; loop the ending into the opening beat.
- Duck music only under actual speech; otherwise full bed.

## recap_voiceover
- No face: b-roll/footage carries 100% of the visual channel; narration drives cut points
  (cut on sentence boundaries ±3 frames).
- Cadence 2–4s (education pace); captions word-by-word or 3-word phrases, large, centered.
- Music bed −20 LUFS under the VO; one SFX per scene change maximum.
- Every claim in the VO gets a matching visual within 15 frames (see it while hearing it).
